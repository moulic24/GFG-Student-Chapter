Event Highlights:

1.Registration and Participation:

The "Coder Thing" coding contest, organized by GeeksforGeeks VIIT, witnessed an overwhelming response with 1,728 students registering for the event. Among them, 1,124 students actively participated, showcasing their coding prowess.

2.Contest Structure:

The contest was designed with multiple coding challenges, testing participants' problem-solving abilities and coding skills. It featured a diverse set of problems, covering various domains of computer science.

3.Enthusiastic Participation:

The participants showed great enthusiasm throughout the contest, competing with dedication and determination to solve the challenges within the stipulated time.

4. Learning and Skill Development:

The coding contest provided a platform for students to enhance their coding skills, learn new algorithms, and gain exposure to real-world programming challenges.

5.Recognition and Rewards:

The top performers were recognized and rewarded for their outstanding performance. Prizes were awarded to the winners as a token of appreciation.

The "Coder Thing" coding contest was a resounding success, engaging a significant percentage of students from VIIT's tech community. The event offered a competitive yet friendly environment, fostering a spirit of healthy competition and mutual learning.

The GeeksforGeeks VIIT team expressed gratitude to all the participants for making the contest a grand success. They highlighted the importance of continuous learning, practicing, and participating in such contests to sharpen coding skills and prepare for future challenges.

The Coder Thing coding contest concluded on a high note, leaving participants motivated to explore more opportunities in the world of coding and programming. The event served as a stepping stone for many aspiring coders towards achieving excellence in their coding journey.
